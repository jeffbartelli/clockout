SSI Retirement Benefit

Nearly all U.S. citizens are entitled to Social Security. If you have worked in the U.S. and payed taxes then you have contributed to Social Security.

To determine what your estimated monthly payout will be you can do one of two things: use this calculator provided by the Social Security Administration to receive an estimate of your monthly benefit; or, you can retrieve an accurate estimate from your Social Security account. To do this, go to this site to set up an account and access all of your Social Security contribution data.

You must also select an age to begin receiving Social Security benefits. These benefits cannot be claimed before the age of 62, at which point you will receive 70% of your benefit. Full benefits are received if you retire at age 67. And if you delay receiving benefits until the age of 70, you will receive 124% of your estimated benefit.

Note: The Social Security Administration estimates that after 2035 it will only collect enough payroll taxes to pay out 80% of your estimated benefit.

Traditional IRAs

The Traditional IRA is an Individual Retirement Account that allows you to make tax-deferred contributions until the age of 70. You pay standard income taxes on all distributions.This is beneficial if you expect to pay less in income taxes at retirement than you do today.

Maximum annual contributions cannot exceed $6,000, unless you are over 50, in which case you can contribute up to $7,000. For more information visit this site.

Enter the total values for all Traditional Roth IRA accounts you may hold in the fields below.

Roth IRAs

The Roth IRA is an Individual Retirement Account where taxes are taken out of your contribution now so that you avoid paying taxes on the distributions upon payout during retirement. This is beneficial if you expect to pay more in income taxes at retirement than you do today.

Maximum annual contributions cannot exceed $6,000, unless you are over 50, in which case you can contribute up to $7,000. For more information visit this site.

Enter the total values for all Roth IRA accounts you may hold in the fields below.
