Social Security Retirement

Instructions

Enter the full (age 67, 100%) monthly benefit in the field Project Full Montly SSI Benefit field, regardless of which age you plan to apply for SSI benefits. If you plan to retire before or after age 67, select the correct age in the Age dropdown menu. Note that the percentage shown with each age is the amount of SSI benefit you will receive if you initiate benefits at that age.

Details

Nearly all U.S. citizens are entitled to Social Security. If you have worked in the U.S. and payed taxes then you have likely contributed to Social Security.

There are two ways to determine your estmated monthly benefit:

  • The Social Security Administration's Retirement Estimator (easiest option), or
  • A detailed estimate retrieved from your Social Security account

Note: The Social Security Administration estimates that after 2035 it will only collect enough payroll taxes to pay out 80% of your estimated benefit. This tool adjusts anticipated bayouts accordingly.

FERS Pension

Instructions

For your FERS pension, enter the monthly or annual benefit amount you expect to receive at the time of retirement. Also enter the age at which you plan to begin receiving the benefit. Only enter the full retirement benefit amount (the 100% amount received at age 62); if you will begin receiving benefits before 62, this tool will adjust the payout accordingly.

You can find instructions for calculating your FERS benefit here. This calculator can help determine your FERS payout, but requires submitting an email address to receive your results.

Retirement Salary

Instructions

Just because you plan to retire doesn't mean you plan to stop working. If you anticipate having another job after retirement - even if for just a few years or only part time - then enter the details here.

Retirement is likely years away it's hard to estimate how much you will earn, so make a guess of how much you'd like to earn in today's dollars.

Rental Income

Instructions

If you own rental properties - or expect to own rental properties at the time of retirement - enter basic details about them here. Specifically, include the net annual rental profits you anticipate earning (in today's dollars), as well as the anticipated annual rate of profit growth.

Traditional IRAs

Instructions

Enter the total values for ALL Traditional IRA accounts you own in the fields below.

Details

The Traditional IRA is an Individual Retirement Account that allows you to make tax-deferred contributions until the age of 70. Beginning at age 59 1/2, you can begin to make withdrawals from all Traditional IRAs. With Traditional IRAs, you pay standard income taxes on all distributions.

As of 2019, maximum annual contributions to ALL IRAs cannot exceed $6,000. However, at age 50, you can make additional "catch up" contributions of $1,000, raising your total annual contributions to $7,000. Check the option for catch up contributions if you'd like to include these contributions to a Traditional IRA.

For more information visit this site.

Roth IRAs

Instructions

Enter the total values for ALL Roth IRA accounts you own in the fields below.

Details

The Roth IRA is an Individual Retirement Account that allows you to make after-tax contributions until the age of 70. Beginning at age 59 1/2, you can begin to make withdrawals from all Roth IRAs. With Roth IRAs, you never pay taxes on distributions. Also, since contributions to Roth IRAs have already been taxed, you can withdrawal your contributions at any time without penalties.

As of 2019, maximum annual contributions to ALL IRAs cannot exceed $6,000. However, at age 50, you can make additional "catch up" contributions of $1,000, raising your total annual contributions to $7,000. Check the option for catch up contributions if you'd like to include these contributions to a Roth IRA.

For more information visit this site.

Traditional Employee Contribution Plans

Instructions

Populate the fields with totals for all Traditional accounts covered in this category.

Details

Employee Contribution Plans are a type of employer-sponsored savings plan. With these accounts, you can set aside up to $19,500 per year, with your employer usually matching a portion of your contributions. "Catch Up" contributions can be made beginning at the age of 50, raising the total contribution limit for all of these accounts by $6,500, to $26,000 per year.

With Traditional plans, your contributions are tax free, and distributions taken in retirement are taxed as normal income.

The following types of Traditional Accounts should be recorded here:

  • 401k
  • 403b
  • 457b
  • Thrift Savings Plan
  • Single 401k
  • Safe Harbor 401k

Simple IRA

Instructions

Enter details about your Simple IRA here.

Details

Simple IRAs are retirement plans for small business with fewer than 100 employees. Known as Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees (SIMPLE), employers can choose to make a 2% contribution to all employees, or a matching contribution of up to 3%. Employees can contribution up to $13,500 per year. If over 50, you can make an additional "catch up" contribution of up to $3,000. For more information check out this page on Investopedia.
