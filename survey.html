Social Security Retirement

Instructions

Enter your estimated Social Security retirement benefit here. You can enter either the monthly or annual amount. Enter the full (100%) benefit amount.

Select the age at which you want to begin receiving Social Security benefits. You will receive 100% of your benefits if you initiate benefits at the age of 67. You will receive a reduced percentage if you begin receiving benefits before 67. You will receive an increased percentage if you begin receiving benefits after 67. The age dropdown will show you the percentage received for each age.

Details

There are two ways to determine your estmated monthly benefit:

  • The Social Security Administration's Retirement Estimator (easiest option), or
  • A detailed estimate retrieved from your Social Security account

Warning: The Social Security Administration estimates that after 2035 it will only collect enough payroll taxes to provide 80% of your benefit. ClockOut adjusts your anticipated benefits accordingly.

Traditional IRAs

Instructions

Enter the total values for ALL Traditional IRA accounts you own in the fields below.

Details

The Traditional IRA is an Individual Retirement Account that allows you to make tax-deferred contributions until the age of 70. Beginning at age 59 1/2, you can begin withdrawing from your Traditional IRA, though you will pay standard income taxes on all withdrawals.

As of 2019, maximum annual contributions to All IRAs you hold cannot exceed $6,000. Beginning at age 50, you can make additional "catch up" contributions of $1,000, raising your total annual contribution limit to $7,000. Check the option for catch up contributions if you'd like to include in your Traditional IRA.

For more information visit this site.

Roth IRAs

Instructions

Enter the total amounts for all of your Roth IRA accounts.

Details

The Roth IRA is an Individual Retirement Account that allows you to make after-tax contributions until the age of 70. Beginning at age 59 1/2, you can make tax-free withdrawals from your Roth IRA. Since Roth IRA contributions have already been taxed, you can withdraw your principal amount at anytime without penalty.

As of 2019, maximum annual contributions to ALL IRAs cannot exceed $6,000. However, at age 50, you can make additional "catch up" contributions of $1,000, raising your total annual contributions to $7,000. Check the option for catch up contributions if you'd like to include them in your Roth IRA.

For more information visit this site.

Traditional Employee Contribution Plans

Instructions

Enter the total amounts for all traditional employee contribution accounts covered in this category. If you enter a percentage, the dollar amount will auto-populate.

Details

Employee Contribution Plans are a type of employer-sponsored savings plan. With these accounts, you can contribute up to $19,500 per year. Your employer will usually match your contributions up to a specified limit, which legally cannot exceed $56,000. At the age of 50, you can begin making "Catch Up" contributions of up to $6,500, raising your annual contribution limit to $26,000 per year.

With Traditional plans, your contributions are tax free, and distributions taken in retirement are taxed as normal income.

The following types of Traditional Accounts should be recorded here:

  • 401k
  • 403b
  • 457b
  • Thrift Savings Plan
  • Single 401k
  • Safe Harbor 401k

Roth Employee Contribution Plans

Instructions

Enter the total amounts for all Roth employee contribution accounts covered in this category. If you enter a percentage, the dollar amount will auto-populate.

Details

Employee Contribution Plans are a type of employer-sponsored savings plan. With these accounts, you can contribute up to $19,500 per year. Your employer may match your contributions, up to a certain limit, though employer contributions to a Roth account will be taxable. Your employer's contribution cannot exceed $56,000. At the age of 50, you can begin making "Catch Up" contributions of up to $6,500, raising your annual contribution limit for all employee contribution accounts to $26,000 per year.

With designated Roth plans, you contribute after you've paid taxes. The benefit of this is that all distributions taken in retirement are tax free.

The following types of designated Roth Accounts should be recorded here:

  • 401k
  • 403b
  • 457b
  • Thrift Savings Plan

Simple IRA

Instructions

Enter details about your Simple IRA here. If you enter a percentage, the dollar amount will auto-populate.

Details

Simple IRAs are retirement plans for small business with fewer than 100 employees. Known as Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees (SIMPLE), employers can choose to make a 2% contribution to all employees, or a matching contribution of up to 3%. Employees can contribution up to $13,500 per year. If over 50, you can make an additional "catch up" contribution of up to $3,000. For more information check out this page on Investopedia.
